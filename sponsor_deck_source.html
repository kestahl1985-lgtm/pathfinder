01 · Introduction

A free career-guidance tool for every South African — paid for by the institutions who want to meet them.

Vula is a WhatsApp-native career assessment for anyone in South Africa looking for career direction. In under five minutes they answer 30 tappable questions and receive a personalised profile, the qualifications each path requires, and a downloadable report.

Vula was built first for school learners facing subject choices. It became clear the same question — what am I actually suited to? — is asked just as often by someone mid-career, retrenched, or returning to work. There is no upper age limit.

It is free to the user, permanently. Revenue comes from institutions who sponsor visibility of their courses inside those results — matched to interests and province.

What we're asking for

A sponsorship partnership. Your courses shown to learners whose profile and province genuinely match what you offer — at the moment they are choosing.

What we are not asking for

Nothing that involves buying learner data. Vula never transfers a learner's personal information to a sponsor. There is no database for you to secure.

02 · The problem

Learners choose blind at fifteen, and find out at eighteen.

Most South African schools have one career counsellor for hundreds of learners, if any at all. Life Orientation teachers are rarely trained career counsellors, and the subject-choice conversation often does not happen.

So learners lock in Grade 9 and 10 subject choices with almost no real information about what those choices open or close off. Drop Maths, and engineering, actuarial science and most of health sciences quietly close — years before anyone says so out loud.

What this costs an institution

By the time your recruitment team reaches a learner in Grade 12, a large share of them no longer qualify for your programmes — and never knew they were disqualifying themselves.

Open days, print and digital campaigns all arrive after the decision that mattered. The subject-choice window is the last point at which the outcome is still open.

04 · The solution

Vula Career Guide

A career assessment that meets learners where they already are, costs them nothing, and gives them something concrete to act on.

  1. Free, permanently. No fee, no subscription, no paywall at the result.
  2. No app and no login. It runs entirely inside WhatsApp, on any phone, using almost no data.
  3. Evidence-based profiling. A 30-question RIASEC interest inventory — the standard framework in career psychology.
  4. Actionable output. Matched careers, the subjects and marks each requires, and qualification pathways — not just a personality label.
  5. Privacy by design. POPIA consent before any data is collected, parental-consent notice for minors, and DELETE honoured immediately.

Where a sponsor appears

When a learner opens a career that matches your programme — and they are in your province — your institution and course are shown inside that result, with a link and prospectus.

It is the moment they are actively exploring that path. Not a banner, and not an interruption.

The channel is not a detail. It decides who we can reach at all.

Every other way of delivering career guidance filters out precisely the people who have none — those in under-resourced schools, on entry-level or shared phones, buying data by the gigabyte.

What this buys the learner

No install, no account, no app store. The whole assessment is about 36 short text messages and a 5 KB report — roughly 25 KB, or 0.2 cents of prepaid data at R79/GB. A single university web page typically costs a visitor more. It runs in four languages: English, isiZulu, isiXhosa and Afrikaans.

The honest trade-off

Building on WhatsApp means depending on Meta's platform rules, and messaging carries a per-conversation cost that scales with learner volume. We accept both. Neither changes the fact that it is the only channel that reaches the learners this is for.

08 · The learner journey

Five minutes, start to finish.

  1. The learner messages Vula on WhatsApp — from a poster, a QR code, a teacher, or a friend forwarding the number.
  2. Consent and language first. POPIA consent is taken before any data is collected, and the learner picks their language.
  3. Name, age and province. Grade is not asked, and there is no upper age limit. Province and assessment outcomes are what drive sponsor matching.
  4. Thirty interest questions. Tappable, no typing, scored across the six RIASEC traits.
  5. Results and report. Matched careers with required subjects and pathways, a PDF report, and where they fit, sponsor courses.
  6. They reply INFO and stay inside WhatsApp. Entrance requirements, subjects, duration and how to apply come back as a message. Your prospectus arrives as a WhatsApp document — never a link to a data-heavy web page.

The sponsor moment

At step five, when someone opens a specific career, Vula checks whether any sponsor's course matches both that career's interest profile and their province. If yours does, it appears there.

Nothing forces them out of WhatsApp. Requirements and your prospectus are delivered in-chat, because data cost is the single biggest reason this audience abandons a funnel.

They decide whether to follow up. Nothing about them is sent to you.

13 · Questions we get asked

The commercial questions.

How many people will actually see our courses?
Your floor is in the contract: 150 on Faculty, 400 on Provincial, 1,600 on National — distinct people, not page views. That is R120 per learner reached at every tier. Fall short and your term extends free. We will not quote a forecast above the floor: we have no delivery history yet, and an invented number would be worth nothing to you.
What if you never get the audience?
Founding sponsors can cancel at day 90 with a pro-rata refund if we have not reached a quarter of the floor (clause 3.6). Your exposure is capped at one quarter of the fee. We also cap how many sponsors we take per province — placements rotate through a finite pool, so overselling a province would put everyone's floor at risk, including yours.
Another university sponsors the same course in our province. Who gets shown?
Both of you, in rotation. Where two sponsors match a learner comparably, the one who has reached fewer learners in the last 30 days is shown. Nobody is starved by being second to sign, and match quality is never traded for fairness — a weak match is still not shown.
Can we test with one faculty before committing?
Yes. The faculty tier is R18,000/year for a single faculty or programme in one province. Courses are tagged individually, so a Humanities-only placement is set up the same day.
What does 20% off annual actually mean?
Provincial is R5,000/month or R48,000/year. National is R20,000/month or R192,000/year. Annual is payable in advance. Monthly is available at the listed rate with no lock-in beyond the term.
Why is national 4× provincial and not 9×?
Because you are buying audience, not provinces. National reaches 4.2× the learners of KwaZulu-Natal, the largest province, for 4.0× the price. Buying the top three separately costs R144,000 for 56% of the market.
How many other sponsors will be in our province?
Few, by design. Every floor we sell is a claim on the same pool of learners, so we track committed floors against actual reach per province and stop selling into a province before existing commitments are at risk. We also prefer sponsors in different faculty areas, who compete for different careers.
Can we buy exclusivity in our province?
No, and deliberately. We priced an exclusive tier and withdrew it before going to market, because nothing in the matching logic could have enforced it. We would rather not sell a promise the platform cannot keep.
Can you target a city or a campus catchment?
Not today. Matching is by province, because province is the only location we ask for — asking a fifteen-year-old for a suburb collects more personal data than the guidance requires. Finer targeting would mean collecting more, and we would rather not.

14 · Questions we get asked

Data, compliance and reporting.

Do we receive learner contact details?
No, and there is no upgrade that changes this. Vula never transfers a learner's personal information to a sponsor. You get counts, never people. If someone wants to reach you, they do it themselves using the link and contact details you supply.
Then how does a lead actually reach us?
The learner replies INFO and receives your entrance requirements, subjects, duration and contact details inside WhatsApp, plus your prospectus as a document. They approach you directly, already knowing what they need.
What is our POPIA exposure?
Effectively none from this arrangement. You never become a second custodian of a minor's data because none is transferred. Vula takes POPIA consent before collecting anything, carries a parental-consent notice for under-18s, and honours DELETE immediately.
How is an "impression" counted?
As a distinct person. If one learner opens your placement six times, that is one learner reached, not six. Raw view counts are reported separately for engagement, but the contractual number is people.
What reporting do we get, and how often?
Learners reached per course, at least quarterly (clause 3.5), plus pacing against your floor so under-delivery is visible while there is still term left to fix it — not discovered at the end.
Is the assessment scientifically credible?
It uses RIASEC (Holland Codes), among the most-studied frameworks in vocational psychology. Appendix B sets out the evidence and its limits — these are modest effect sizes and we say so. Appendix A explains exactly how scoring and matching work.
Does this qualify for our B-BBEE scorecard?
We do not claim it does. Socio-Economic Development contributions are meant to be grant-like, and a sponsorship that returns brand visibility to you may well be treated as marketing spend. Ask your B-BBEE advisor. If a split structure works, we will happily accommodate it.
What happens at renewal?
Terms are 12 months and renew automatically unless either side gives 30 days' notice. Founding sponsors hold their signing rate at renewal.

Appendix A

How the assessment works.

Vula uses RIASEC, the Holland Codes: a model sorting work interests into six types. Nobody is one type — everyone scores on all six, and the shape of that spread is what points toward fitting work.

Holland typeHow Vula words itWhat it looks like in practice
RRealisticHands-on & practicalBuilding, fixing, operating machinery, working outdoors or with your hands
IInvestigativeCurious & analyticalResearching, diagnosing, working out why something behaves the way it does
AArtisticCreative & expressiveDesigning, writing, performing, producing original work
SSocialCaring & people-focusedTeaching, nursing, counselling, community and care work
EEnterprisingDriven & enterprisingLeading, persuading, selling, starting and running things
CConventionalOrganised & detail-focusedRecords, systems, accuracy, finance and administration

Scoring

Thirty questions, five per type, asked in rotation. Each is answered No, Maybe or Yes — worth 0, 1 or 2 — so every type scores out of 10. The three highest come back as that person's strengths.

Matching

Each of the 88 careers carries a Holland interest code. A person's full six-score profile is compared to every career by hexagonal similarity — careers near their interests on the Holland hexagon score highest, opposite ones near zero. The six best-fitting are returned, and sponsor courses are scored on the same scale.

Appendix B

The evidence base for the assessment method.

RIASEC is among the most-studied frameworks in vocational psychology. Included for completeness, with its limits stated. Appendix A explains how the assessment itself works.

r = .23
Interests predict academic performance at this level, with job performance similar when the criterion is carefully measured.Nye, Su, Rounds & Drasgow (2012), meta-analysis spanning 60+ years of research
r = .17
Interest–environment congruence predicts job satisfaction, rising to about .25 when the environment is measured independently rather than inferred from a job title.Tsabari, Tziner & Meir (2005), meta-analysis of 53 studies
—
Congruence predicts persistence in a chosen college major or occupation; people who change jobs frequently move toward better-fitting ones.Nauta (2010), review of Holland's theory

Read honestly, these are modest effects

Interest-fit explains roughly 3–6% of the variance in job satisfaction. Pay, autonomy and management quality all matter more. Interest profiling is not destiny, and we will not claim it is.

The relevant comparison is not RIASEC against perfect guidance. It is RIASEC against what most South African learners have today, which is nothing at all.

Vula's own delivery is not yet measured. The instrument is validated; Vula's application of it will be evidenced once there is usage data, not before.
