# Vula Bursary & Funding Research — Draft (July 2026)

**Status: DRAFT — not yet in production code or shown to learners. Needs a second verification pass (each provider's own current page) before being presented as fact, per the same standard already applied to CAREER_RESEARCH_DRAFT.md.**

This document consolidates research on bursary and funding opportunities for South African
high school learners heading into tertiary study, gathered via live web search in July 2026
(not from memory) — specifically because bursary deadlines, income thresholds, and subject
requirements change annually and a wrong figure could cause a student to miss a real deadline
or apply to the wrong programme.

**How to read this:** each entry is dated to this research session. Bursary programmes
re-open on an annual cycle (most for the following year's intake), so **treat every deadline
below as indicative of the cycle, not a fixed date** — always confirm the live date on the
provider's own page before advising a student. Entries note South Africa's academic-year
numbering convention: a "2027 Bursary" funds study starting in the 2027 academic year, and
applications for it typically open partway through 2026.

## Foundational / means-tested funding (relevant to every career)

### NSFAS (National Student Financial Aid Scheme)
- **What it covers**: Full cost of study (tuition, accommodation, meals, learning materials,
  transport allowance) at any public university or public TVET college on NSFAS's institution list.
- **Eligibility**: SA citizen or permanent resident with a valid ID; combined household income
  **≤ R350,000/year** (raised to **≤ R600,000/year for applicants living with a disability**);
  confirmed acceptance or current enrolment at a qualifying public institution.
- **2027 cycle**: Online applications open **1 September 2026**, close **15 November 2026**.
- **Confidence**: High — figures consistent across NSFAS's own site and the official eligibility
  policy PDF.

### ISFAP (Ikusasa Student Financial Aid Programme)
- **What it is**: A public–private bursary specifically for the **"missing middle"** — households
  that earn too much for NSFAS but still can't afford university.
- **Eligibility**: Household income **R0 – R600,000/year**; accepted at one of **26 partner public
  universities** (includes UCT, Wits, UP, Stellenbosch, UKZN, UJ, Nelson Mandela University and
  others). Applicant must first apply and be accepted to a university/programme funded by ISFAP —
  not all degrees at every partner university are covered.
- **2027 cycle**: Applications close **31 October 2026**; anything submitted after that rolls to
  the 2028 cycle.
- **Confidence**: High on income band and university-partner model; **Medium on which specific
  degrees/universities are funded in a given year** — this varies and needs checking per student's
  actual course choice at isfap.org.za.

### Funza Lushaka Bursary (teaching)
- Covered in depth already in `CAREER_RESEARCH_DRAFT.md`'s Teacher entry. Key figures reconfirmed
  this session: full-cost bursary (tuition, accommodation, meals, books, allowance) for a BEd or
  PGCE at a public university, in exchange for **one year of public-school teaching service per
  year funded**. Priority subjects: Mathematics, Science, Technology, official languages, and
  Special Needs Education, with phase-specific priority lists (e.g. FET phase priorities include
  Accounting, Agricultural Sciences, Civil/Electrical Technology, EGD, Economics).
- **2027 cycle**: Opens **1 October 2026**. (2026 cycle for reference: opened 7 October 2025,
  closed 24 January 2026 for new applicants — expect a similar ~4-month window.)
- **Confidence**: High — DBE's own site and application-notes PDF are consistent.

### Motsepe Foundation Bursary
- **What it covers**: Undergraduate study at a recognised SA tertiary institution, strong STEM
  emphasis but also covers Agriculture, Arts, Business Studies, IT, and Medicine.
- **Eligibility**: Academically strong, financially disadvantaged SA learners.
- **Cycle**: Annual window that has typically closed in **early June**.
- **Confidence**: Medium — clear on scope and field breadth, exact income-threshold figure not
  independently confirmed this session, verify at the Motsepe Foundation's own site.

## Engineering, mining & technical trades

### Eskom Bursary (University + University of Technology tracks)
- **Subjects**: University (BEng/BSc) track — English L4, Pure Mathematics L5, Physical Science
  L5. University of Technology (Diploma) track — English L4, Pure Mathematics L4, Physical
  Science L4. Grade 11 year-end results are considered for initial shortlisting, final selection
  depends on actual matric results.
- **Fields**: Chemical, Civil, Computer, Electrical (Light/Heavy current), Electromechanical/
  Mechatronics, Industrial, Mechanical, and Metallurgy Engineering.
- **Other conditions**: SA citizen; conditional acceptance at a recognised public university;
  may not hold another bursary simultaneously.
- **Scale**: 2026 intake offered 138 bursaries (79 university, 59 UoT) — gives a sense of typical
  volume, not a guaranteed number for future intakes.
- **Cycle note**: 2026 intake closed 14 November 2025 — 2027 intake not yet open as of this
  research session; watch Eskom's careers portal.
- **Confidence**: High on subject-level thresholds — Eskom's own bursary pages are specific and
  internally consistent.

### Transnet Bursary (Engineering & Technical)
- **Subjects**: Mathematics, Physical Science, and English all at **L5 (60%+)**.
- **Fields**: Industrial, Electrical, Electronic, Mechanical, Metallurgy, Computer, Civil,
  Electro-mechanical, Mechatronics, Chemical Engineering.
- **Process**: Interview at a Transnet Academy (Johannesburg, Bloemfontein, Cape Town, Durban) +
  medical fitness assessment for shortlisted candidates.
- **Service obligation**: Post-graduation work-back at Transnet, matched to years funded
  (subject to job availability).
- **Confidence**: Medium-High — subject thresholds consistent across sources; exact intake
  numbers/closing dates fluctuate year to year, confirm current cycle at transnet.net.

### Exxaro Bursary (incl. Feeder Scheme + disability-specific stream)
- **What it covers**: Registration, tuition, books, residence accommodation, meal allowance, cash
  allowance, and in some years a laptop/data allowance.
- **Fields**: Mining-relevant engineering (electrical, mechanical/mechatronic, mining engineering)
  under the Feeder Scheme; a separate stream is specifically open to **students living with a
  disability**, in any field of study, minimum 3-year diploma or degree.
- **Confidence**: Medium — scope confirmed across multiple sources, exact current-year subject/
  mark thresholds not independently verified this session, check exxaro.com.

### Anglo American Bursary (incl. Anglo American Platinum, De Beers, Kumba Iron Ore streams)
- **What it covers**: Top-performing learners with an interest in fields relevant to mining —
  Mining Engineering, other Engineering disciplines, Process, Geosciences, and Finance.
- **Structure note**: Anglo American runs this through several operating-company streams
  (Anglo American Platinum, De Beers, Kumba Iron Ore) which may have slightly different
  application portals/criteria per commodity business — treat as one family of bursaries, not
  a single uniform programme.
- **Confidence**: Medium — consistent on field scope; check angloamerican.com (from ~mid-2026 for
  a 2027-intake cycle) for the specific current subject/mark requirements per stream.

### merSETA / other SETA-funded artisan and trade routes
- Already covered structurally in `CAREER_RESEARCH_DRAFT.md` (learnerships + Trade Test route for
  Electrician, Motor Mechanic, Plumber, Boilermaker, Welder, Fitter & Turner, etc.) — these are
  typically **stipend-paid workplace learnerships**, not classic tuition bursaries, funded via the
  relevant SETA (merSETA, CETA) rather than a single named bursary. No new figures found this
  session; this remains a gap consistent with the career draft's existing notes.

## Information technology & telecoms

### MTN Foundation Bursary
- **Fields**: ICT, Engineering, Commerce (MTN's stated skills priorities).
- **Eligibility**: Academically strong SA students from previously disadvantaged backgrounds;
  **minimum 65% average** cited.
- **Cycle**: Application window typically **June–August**.
- **Confidence**: Medium — field scope and average-mark figure found consistently, exact
  income-threshold not independently confirmed.

### Vodacom Bursary
- **Fields**: STEM broadly, with emphasis on disciplines feeding the ICT industry, plus Business
  and Commerce.
- **What it covers**: Full first/second/third-year funding at a recognised SA university, plus a
  laptop and cellphone, vacation-work placements, and work-readiness training for final-year
  students.
- **Selection basis**: Merit-based.
- **Confidence**: Medium-High — Vodacom's own bursary page is the primary source and is specific
  about the non-monetary benefits (device provision, vacation work).

### Telkom Bursary
- **Fields**: Electrical Engineering, IT, Computer Science, Marketing, Accounting.
- **2027 cycle**: Closing date **31 July 2026**.
- **Confidence**: Medium — consistent across sources, exact subject/mark thresholds not
  independently confirmed this session.

## Health sciences

### Netcare Bursary Programme
- **Fields**: Medicine, Nursing, Pharmacy, and other health sciences.
- **What it covers**: Tuition, prescribed textbooks, accommodation, and a living allowance.
- **Eligibility**: SA citizenship/valid residency; matric (NSC); strong English; willingness to
  work shifts and complete clinical placements; **Life Sciences flagged as especially important
  for nursing applicants specifically**; means-tested (parent/guardian proof of income required).
- **Application**: Direct email to education@netcare.co.za, not a general online portal.
- **Confidence**: Medium-High — Netcare's own site is the primary source; the "especially
  important" phrasing for Life Sciences is a recommendation signal, not confirmed as a hard
  subject gate.

### Provincial Department of Health bursaries (medicine, nursing, pharmacy)
- **Structure**: **Every province runs its own bursary scheme independently** (Western Cape,
  KwaZulu-Natal, Gauteng, Eastern Cape, etc.) — there is no single national application; a
  student must apply to the specific province they're willing to work back in.
- **What it covers**: Often the most substantial package available for health-science students —
  full tuition and commonly a monthly stipend.
- **Service obligation**: Work-back basis, **one year of service per year funded**; community
  service counts as the first payback year.
- **Selection driver**: Tied to that specific province's scarce-skills needs, which change
  year to year — fields on offer are **not fixed**, re-advertised annually per province.
- **Confidence**: Medium — the work-back structure is consistently described across provinces,
  but exact fields-on-offer and thresholds must be checked per specific province each year (e.g.
  Western Cape's own health-bursary portal at hsdims.westerncape.gov.za).

## Commerce, accounting, actuarial science & finance

### SAICA Thuthuka Bursary Fund (Chartered Accountancy)
- **Purpose**: Specifically established to support **disadvantaged African and Coloured
  students** pursuing CA(SA) via a BCom Accounting (CA-stream) degree — this is a targeted-equity
  programme, not open to all applicants regardless of background.
- **Subjects**: **Pure Mathematics ≥ 60%** — Mathematics Literacy and Technical Mathematics
  explicitly **do not qualify**.
- **Financial threshold**: Combined family income **≤ R350,000/year** (same NSFAS-aligned figure).
- **Process**: Must write the **National Benchmark Test (NBT)** before end of August, or submit
  results if already written.
- **What it covers**: Tuition, accommodation, meals, books, transport, monthly allowance, plus
  ongoing academic and psychosocial support through the degree.
- **2027 cycle**: Applications close **31 August 2026, 11:59am**.
- **Confidence**: High — SAICA's own page and application brochure are specific and consistent.
  This is a strong, concrete complement to the Accountant entry already in
  `CAREER_RESEARCH_DRAFT.md`.

### Old Mutual Actuarial Science Bursary
- **Subjects**: Demanding — **Mathematics ≥ 80%**, English HL ≥ 60% (or FAL ≥ 70%), all other
  subjects ≥ 70%. Consistent with the career draft's existing "most maths-intensive career on the
  list" framing for Actuary.
- **What it covers**: Tuition, study materials, meals, residence accommodation, return flights
  home if studying away from hometown, vacation work, networking, and **guaranteed employment at
  Old Mutual post-qualification**.
- **Universities**: UCT, Stellenbosch, UP, Wits, UFS, NWU, UJ, UKZN.
- **2027 cycle**: Closes **30 June 2026**.
- **Confidence**: High — figures specific and internally consistent with Old Mutual's own page.

### Investec Bursary (Actuarial Science + other commerce fields)
- **Structure**: Awarded on **financial need and academic potential**; for Actuarial Science
  specifically, generally targets first-year applicants (exception noted for UCT Business
  Science students).
- **What it covers**: Full cover of study-related expenses.
- **Confidence**: Medium — scope confirmed, exact current subject/mark thresholds not
  independently found this session.

### Law bursaries (LLB)
- **Structure — no single dominant national bursary**: funding for law is spread across many
  smaller providers rather than one flagship programme (unlike Thuthuka for accounting or
  Funza Lushaka for teaching).
- **Law Society of South Africa (LSSA) bursary**: funded from interest earned on attorneys' trust
  accounts; primarily targets **3rd- and 4th-year LLB students** (i.e. not a first-entry bursary
  for matriculants); needs financial need + academic performance; closing date generally around
  **15 August** annually.
- **Law-firm-funded bursaries**: named providers include Adams & Adams, Bowman Gilfillan, Cliffe
  Dekker Hofmeyr, Fasken, HCI Foundation, Investec, LegalWise, Norton Rose Fulbright, Old Mutual
  Education Trust, and Webber Wentzel — each runs its own separate scheme with its own criteria.
- **Cycle pattern**: Most law bursaries for a given intake year open around **May** and close by
  **September** of the preceding year.
- **Confidence**: Medium — the "many small providers, no single flagship" structure is well
  corroborated, but this means a Vula user interested in law needs to be pointed at a *list* to
  shop, not a single named bursary the way Thuthuka works for accounting. Individual firms'
  current-year criteria were not verified line-by-line this session.

## Agriculture

### Land Bank External Bursary Programme
- **What it covers**: Full tuition, prescribed books, monthly living allowance, and (where
  applicable) accommodation/meal allowances.
- **Scope**: Undergraduate Agriculture at Agricultural Colleges registered with the Council on
  Higher Education (CHE) — this is narrower than a general "any agriculture degree" bursary,
  worth flagging since it doesn't cover every route into Agricultural/Farm Manager (an existing
  `CAREER_RESEARCH_DRAFT.md` entry already lists Elsenburg/Cedara/Tsolo-style colleges and BSc
  Agriculture as two separate pathways — Land Bank's bursary appears tied to the college route).
- **Confidence**: Medium — scope and coverage list clear from Land Bank's own page; not verified
  whether BSc Agriculture at a university also qualifies.

### Motsepe Foundation Bursary
- Already covered above under Foundational funding — Agriculture is explicitly one of its listed
  fields.

## Holistic / "missing middle" support programmes (carried over from the July 2026 career-guide research pass, re-flagged here since they're funding-specific)

These were already surfaced in `CAREER_RESEARCH_DRAFT.md`'s July 2026 update from the Kagiso
Trust Career Guide — repeated here for completeness of this bursary-specific document, not
re-verified independently this session (see that document's sourcing note):

- **Cyril Ramaphosa Education Trust (CRET)** — holistic support (funding + mentorship +
  personal/social development guidance); deliberately targets students often overlooked by
  top-achiever-only bursary programmes.
- **Moshal Scholarship Program** — operates in South Africa and Israel; targets highly motivated
  students doing complex degrees who wouldn't otherwise have access; selection based on potential
  and resilience, not just marks.
- **Allan Gray Orbis Foundation Fellowship** — entrepreneurship-focused, not a pure academic
  bursary. Concrete Grade 11 entry bar: **60% in Pure Mathematics OR 80% in Mathematical
  Literacy**, plus a **70% average** (excluding Life Orientation), under-21, SA citizen, intending
  Commerce/Science/Engineering/Law/Humanities/Arts/Health Science (medicine, veterinary science,
  dentistry explicitly excluded) at WITS, UJ, UCT, NMU, RU, UWC, SU, UP, UFS, or TSiBA. 2025-cycle
  dates found: submit by 30 April, interviews July, selection camp September — treat as a
  template for cycle timing, confirm current year.
- **CSIR Studentship Programme** — funds Science/Engineering/Technology students with research
  potential; includes mentorship and practical experience.
- **Sasol Bursary** (Foundation + non-STEM streams) — STEM-focused Foundation stream plus a
  separate non-STEM stream specifically for candidates from Sasol's fence-line communities,
  children of Sasol Khanyisa shareholders, and children of Sasol employees.
- **IDC (Industrial Development Corporation) Bursary** — targets qualifications addressing
  identified skills shortages across industries; check idc.co.za for the current supported list.
- **TSiBA Business School** — private, not-for-profit, purpose-driven business education; distinct
  from a bursary provider, more a low-cost/funded institution option itself.

## What this research did NOT resolve — flag before using with students

1. **Most corporate bursaries' exact current-year subject/mark thresholds** (Exxaro, Anglo
   American streams, MTN, Vodacom, Telkom, Investec) were confirmed on *scope and field* but not
   independently verified down to the exact percentage for the specific upcoming intake — these
   move year to year and should be checked on the provider's own page before quoting a number to
   a student.
2. **Law bursaries** are the weakest-covered field in this pass — no single flagship programme
   exists the way Thuthuka does for accounting; a real "law bursary" feature would need a proper
   curated shortlist rather than one named answer.
3. **SETA/artisan-trade stipend programmes** (electrician, mechanic, plumber, welder, etc.) are
   structurally different from degree bursaries (workplace learnerships, not tuition funding) and
   weren't re-researched in depth this session — the existing gaps already flagged in
   `CAREER_RESEARCH_DRAFT.md` for those trades' entry requirements still stand.
4. **Provincial Health Department bursaries**: only the Western Cape's portal was directly
   surfaced; the other 8 provinces each run independent schemes not individually checked this
   session.
5. No bursary specific to several Vula career-list entries was found this session: Psychologist,
   Social Worker, Journalist, most Artistic-sector careers (Graphic Designer, Architect, Musician,
   Actor, Fashion Designer, Photographer), and most Conventional-sector careers (Bookkeeper,
   Payroll Administrator, Librarian, Municipal Administrator, Court Clerk) — either genuinely
   under-funded fields, or bursaries exist but weren't surfaced by these particular searches;
   worth a dedicated follow-up pass if Vula wants full career-list coverage.

## Addendum — zabursaries.co.za (user-supplied, checked same session)

Checked as a candidate **aggregator/go-to reference** rather than mined line-by-line — it's a
listings site, not a primary source, so treat anything specific found there the same way as the
other secondary aggregators already flagged throughout this document (verify against the actual
provider before quoting a figure). It's organized into 13 field categories (Accounting, Arts,
Commerce, Computer Science & IT, Construction & Built Environment, Education, Engineering,
General, Government, Law, MBA & Postgraduate, Medical, Science), which is a reasonable structure
to mirror if Vula ever builds a full bursary-browsing feature rather than per-career attachments.

Two genuine gaps from this document's earlier pass that it partially fills:
- **Architecture / Construction & Built Environment** — an entire category this session's
  targeted searches missed (Architecture was one of the "no bursary found" entries above).
- **Renewable-energy community bursaries** — e.g. wind/solar farm-linked programmes (Kangnas,
  Roggeveld cited) that fund students from the surrounding community as part of the project's
  social/economic development obligations. Not yet mapped to a specific Vula career — closest
  fit is probably Engineer or Environmental Scientist — worth a closer look given it's a
  funding *type* (community-linked project funding) not covered anywhere else in this draft.
- Also confirms mining-company bursaries beyond Anglo American/Exxaro/De Beers already covered:
  **Glencore, Kumba, Northam Platinum** each reportedly run their own schemes — not
  independently verified this session.

## Recommended next steps

1. **Do not surface specific bursary deadlines/thresholds to learners until each is re-confirmed
   on the provider's own current page** — same standard as the career data, since a wrong
   deadline is actively harmful (a missed application), not just imprecise.
2. If Vula builds a bursary-matching feature (raised as a product idea this session), the natural
   shape is per-career, not a generic list: attach 1-3 relevant bursaries to each career entry in
   `lib/careers.js` once that schema is restructured (already planned per the career draft's
   "Recommended next steps").
3. Close the 5 uncovered-fields gap above with a second, more targeted research pass before
   claiming full coverage across Vula's 30 (soon 71) careers.
4. Adopt the same **"last verified" date per bursary** discipline already recommended for
   careers — bursary cycles are, if anything, more time-sensitive than subject/APS requirements.
