# Vula Career & Subject Requirements — Research Draft (July 2026)

**Status: DRAFT — not yet in production code. Requires career counsellor / professional body review before use.**

This document consolidates deep research on 71 careers for South African high school
learners, covering RIASEC fit, required NSC (CAPS) subjects, qualification pathways,
and relevant professional bodies. Research was conducted via live web search against
current South African sources (university admission pages, professional body sites,
TVET/SETA documentation, government careers portals) — not generated from memory —
specifically because a wrong subject recommendation could steer a real student away
from subjects they needed.

**How to read the confidence flags:** each career below is marked High / Medium / Low
confidence based on how authoritative and current the sources found were. Several
entries have specific caveats (e.g. "verify against target university's current
prospectus") — these are not hedge-language filler, they reflect genuine gaps or
year-to-year variability the research surfaced. Do not treat any single percentage/APS
figure as universal — South African university admission requirements are set
per-institution and change annually.

**Universal cross-cutting finding:** Mathematics vs Mathematics Literacy is the single
highest-stakes distinction across almost this entire list. Where a career requires
"Mathematics," this means *Pure Mathematics* — Mathematics Literacy explicitly
disqualifies a student from that pathway in most cases. Notable exceptions where Maths
Literacy is genuinely accepted are called out explicitly per career below (e.g. Chef,
Social Work, Police, some Landscaping/Horticulture routes).

## Sector 1: Hands-on / Practical (Realistic)

### Engineer (Civil/Mechanical/Electrical)
- RIASEC: Realistic/Investigative
- Subjects: Mathematics (Pure, not Lit) Level 5-6; Physical Sciences Level 5; English Level 4-5
- Pathway: BEng/BSc(Eng) 4yr (university) → Pr Eng, OR BEngTech 3-4yr (UoT) → Pr Tech Eng, OR National Diploma 3yr → Pr Techni Eng
- Body: ECSA (Engineering Council of South Africa)
- Confidence: High on subjects/structure; Medium on exact APS (varies by university/year)

### Electrician
- RIASEC: Realistic
- Subjects: No fixed NSC gate at entry (most enter via N2/NCV); strongly recommend Mathematics (Pure) + Physical Sciences as foundation
- Pathway: Learnership/apprenticeship (merSETA) + Trade Test (NAMB) → Red Seal; must also register as Registered Person/Wireman to issue COCs
- Body: NAMB (trade test); Dept of Employment and Labour (COC registration)
- Confidence: High on pathway; Medium on precise non-N-course NSC entry requirements

### Motor Mechanic
- RIASEC: Realistic
- Subjects: Mathematics (Pure) + Physical Sciences, 50%+ recommended; N2/NCV alternative entry
- Pathway: N1-N3 or NCV + apprenticeship (~3yr) → Trade Test → Red Seal
- Body: NAMB/QCTO; merSETA
- Confidence: Medium-High

### Plumber
- RIASEC: Realistic
- Subjects: **No authoritative universal NSC subject/level requirement found** — genuine gap. Recommend Mathematics (Pure) + Physical Sciences as safe steer.
- Pathway: N1-N3 (CETA) + apprenticeship (3-4yr) → Trade Test → PIRB board assessment → Licensed Plumber
- Body: PIRB (Plumbing Industry Registration Board) — only Licensed Plumbers may issue COCs
- Confidence: High on pathway/PIRB; **Low on specific subject levels — verify with PIRB or target TVET college directly**

### Boilermaker
- RIASEC: Realistic
- Subjects: Mathematics, Physical Sciences, Engineering Graphics & Design, Mechanical Technology recommended
- Pathway: 3yr apprenticeship (structured %-split college/workplace) → Trade Test → Red Seal
- Body: NAMB/QCTO; merSETA
- Confidence: Medium-High

### Welder
- RIASEC: Realistic
- Subjects: Mathematics, Physical Sciences, Civil/Mechanical Technology recommended
- Pathway: N2/NCV/Technical Matric or Grade 9+4yr experience → Trade Test → Red Seal
- Body: NAMB/QCTO; merSETA
- Confidence: Medium-High

### Diesel Mechanic
- RIASEC: Realistic
- Subjects: Mathematics (Pure) + Physical Sciences 50%+; valid driver's licence typically required
- Pathway: N1-N3/NCV + ~3yr apprenticeship → Trade Test → Red Seal
- Body: NAMB/QCTO; merSETA
- Confidence: Medium-High

### Carpenter/Joiner
- RIASEC: Realistic
- Subjects: **No firm NSC subject/level list found** — Mathematics generally beneficial, not confirmed as a strict gate
- Pathway: N2+ qualification + ~2.5yr on-the-job training → Trade Test → Red Seal (or RPL: 4yr documented experience)
- Body: NAMB/QCTO; CETA/merSETA
- Confidence: Medium; **subject specifics are weak — verify with training provider**

### Bricklayer
- RIASEC: Realistic
- Subjects: Technical Grade 12 (Maths, Science) OR N2 OR NCV L3, all + 3yr experience; **Grade 9 + 4yr experience route exists (matric not strictly mandatory)**
- Pathway: merSETA/CETA learnership → Trade Test → Red Seal
- Body: NAMB/QCTO; CETA
- Confidence: Medium — one of the more accessible trades; important to communicate the non-matric route accurately

### Commercial Pilot
- RIASEC: Realistic/Investigative
- Subjects: Mathematics (Pure) + Physical Sciences **recommended, not strictly mandatory** for flight school entry (SACAA ground school teaches theory regardless); full NSC (matric) required to enrol
- Pathway: SACAA-approved flight school (PPL→CPL, often R500k+) independent of NSC subjects; OR university aviation degree (CPUT, Wits) combining academics + flight hours; ATPL + flight hours needed for airline employment
- Body: SACAA (South African Civil Aviation Authority)
- Confidence: Medium-High on "recommended not mandatory" nuance; final say rests with each flight school

### Chef
- RIASEC: Realistic/Artistic
- Subjects: English L4 (50%+); **Mathematics L3 (40%+) OR Mathematics Literacy L4 (50%+) — one of the few careers where Maths Lit is explicitly accepted**, not just tolerated
- Pathway: 1-3yr culinary diploma/certificate (Prue Leith, ICA Chef School, hotel schools) — vocational, aptitude/passion often weighted over academic marks
- Body: None statutory; SACA (SA Chefs Association) is voluntary
- Confidence: High — clear, well-corroborated finding

### Agricultural/Farm Manager
- RIASEC: Realistic/Enterprising
- Subjects: Language L4 (50%+); Mathematics L4 (50%+); at least L3 (40%+) in Physical Science, Life Sciences, OR Agricultural Science
- Pathway: Agricultural College Diploma (Elsenburg, Cedara, Tsolo) 2-3yr for practical management; OR BSc Agriculture 3-4yr for commercial/agribusiness roles
- Body: No mandatory registration for farm management generally; SASAE/SACNASP relevant for agricultural scientists
- Confidence: Medium-High on subjects; genuinely flexible on professional-body requirement (good news for students)

### Game Ranger/Wildlife Manager
- RIASEC: Realistic/Investigative
- Subjects: Varies drastically by track — FGASA field guide short courses accept Maths **Literacy** + Geography or Life Sciences (low bar); university conservation/wildlife degrees need Life Sciences + Geography + typically Pure Mathematics
- Pathway: (a) Field guide route: FGASA-accredited short courses (EcoTraining, Bushwise, SAWC) — fast, lower bar; (b) Conservation route: National Diploma in Nature Conservation OR BSc Zoology/Conservation Ecology 3-4yr
- Body: FGASA (field guides, non-statutory); SACNASP (conservation scientists)
- Confidence: Medium — **ask the student which specific role/level they're aiming for**, the two tracks are very different

### Fitter & Turner
- RIASEC: Realistic
- Subjects: N1 entry: Grade 9 pass Maths+Science; N3 entry: Grade 11 pass Maths+Science, or N2. **Matriculants with 40%+ Maths/Science only need to write 2 of 4 N2 subjects** — worth telling students with decent NSC results.
- Pathway: N1-N2/N3 (Fitting & Machining) + merSETA apprenticeship → Trade Test → Red Seal
- Body: NAMB/QCTO; merSETA
- Confidence: Medium-High — one of the more clearly documented trades

### Refrigeration Technician
- RIASEC: Realistic
- Subjects: merSETA minimum is low (Grade 9 + English/Afrikaans + Maths or Science + 2 others); **no authoritative NSC-specific page found** — genuine gap
- Pathway: N-courses (Air Conditioning & Refrigeration) + merSETA apprenticeship → Trade Test → Red Seal → SARACCA registration (safe handling of refrigerants)
- Body: SARACCA; NAMB/QCTO trade test
- Confidence: Medium on pathway; **Low on precise subject levels**

### Landscaper/Landscape Technician
- RIASEC: Realistic/Artistic
- Subjects: English L3; formula-based: (Maths OR Maths Lit level) + (Life Sciences OR Physical Science level) ≥ 7
- Pathway: Distinguish "landscaper" (hands-on trade, National Certificate: Landscaping) from "Landscape Technologist" (SACLAP-registered professional tier, needs NQF6 Diploma) from "Landscape Architect" (full professional degree)
- Body: SACLAP (statutory, tiered registration); SALI (industry association, non-statutory)
- Confidence: Medium — clarify with student which tier they mean

### Merchant Navy/Marine Officer
- RIASEC: Realistic/Investigative
- Subjects: Mathematics (Pure) + Physical Science described as critical across sources; exact NSC % (e.g. 3 subjects L4/50%+) **could not be independently verified from a primary source**
- Pathway: Grade 12 or N3 (60%+) → SAMSA-accredited maritime academy (DUT/CPUT) → 12mo sea service (National Cadet Programme) → SAMSA oral exam → Certificate of Competency; also requires SAMSA medical/eyesight fitness
- Body: SAMSA (South African Maritime Safety Authority); STCW international framework
- Confidence: Medium — pathway well-confirmed, **exact subject percentages need direct verification with DUT/CPUT/SAMSA**

## Sector 2: Curious / Analytical (Investigative)

### Doctor (Medicine — MBChB)
- RIASEC: Investigative (+ Social)
- Subjects: Mathematics (Pure — Maths Lit/Technical Maths disqualifying), Physical Sciences, English, typically L5 (60%+) at competitive schools. **Life Sciences requirement varies by university**: strict at Stellenbosch/NMU/UFS, not explicit (but recommended) at UP/Wits — this variance is the single most important nuance to flag to students.
- Pathway: MBChB 5-6yr at one of 8 SA medical schools (UCT, Wits, Stellenbosch, UP, UKZN, UFS, SMU, Walter Sisulu). Extremely competitive — realistic competitive APS ~40-42 despite lower stated minimums. NBT (National Benchmark Test) usually required.
- Body: HPCSA; + 1yr internship + 1yr community service before independent practice
- Confidence: Medium-High; UP's official page could not be fetched directly (403) — cross-check exact current cutoffs each admissions cycle

### Data Scientist / IT Specialist
- RIASEC: Investigative
- Subjects: Mathematics (Pure) essentially non-negotiable; Physical Sciences recommended; school IT/CAT helpful but not a formal gate
- Pathway: BSc/BCom (Computer Science, Data Science, Statistics) 3yr; Honours/MSc increasingly expected for "data scientist" title specifically
- Body: None mandatory; IITPSA offers voluntary certification
- Confidence: High — least regulated field on this list, more flexible pathway

### Scientist/Researcher (general)
- RIASEC: Investigative
- Subjects: Mathematics + Physical Sciences (physics/chemistry track) or + Life Sciences (biology track) — varies by specific field
- Pathway: BSc 3yr → Honours 1yr (needed for SACNASP + most research roles) → often MSc/PhD
- Body: SACNASP (South African Council for Natural Scientific Professions)
- Confidence: Medium — broad umbrella category, defer to specific-field entries (Physicist, Environmental Scientist, Geologist) for precision

### Pharmacist (BPharm)
- RIASEC: Investigative
- Subjects: Mathematics, Life Sciences, Physical Sciences, English all typically L5 (60%+) at competitive schools; L4 (50%) cited as absolute floor at less competitive intakes
- Pathway: BPharm 4yr (Wits, UP, Rhodes, UWC, NWU, SMU — must be SAPC-approved) → 1yr internship + 1yr community service
- Body: **SAPC (South African Pharmacy Council) — not HPCSA**, an important distinction not to conflate
- Confidence: High

### Veterinarian (BVSc)
- RIASEC: Investigative (+ Realistic)
- Subjects: **Could not directly confirm exact NSC subject/level table from a primary source** (UP page 403'd, PDF unreadable) — reasonable to infer Mathematics/Physical Sciences/Life Sciences/English all at L5+ given comparable competitiveness to Medicine/Pharmacy, but this is inference, not confirmation
- Pathway: BVSc 6yr — **only offered at UP Onderstepoort, the single vet school in SA**, making this arguably the most competitive programme on the entire list by scarcity of seats alone. Selection interview + SAVC pre-registration required.
- Body: SAVC (South African Veterinary Council)
- Confidence: **Low on subjects — verify directly with UP (vetug@up.ac.za) before giving as fact**

### Actuary
- RIASEC: Investigative
- Subjects: Mathematics (Pure) at a **very high mark** — arguably the most maths-intensive career on the list (some programmes want 80%+ Maths, NSC average 80-85%); English also weighted
- Pathway: BCom/BSc/BBusSc Actuarial Science 3-4yr at one of 7 ASSA-accredited universities (UCT, Wits, Stellenbosch, UP, UJ, UFS, NWU) → ASSA professional exams (Associate → Fellow, 3-8yr post-degree)
- Body: ASSA (Actuarial Society of South Africa)
- Confidence: Medium-High — one of the most academically selective pathways in SA; direct students to the specific university's current published requirements

### Geologist
- RIASEC: Investigative
- Subjects: Mathematics + Physical Sciences L5-6 (60-70%) typically; Life Sciences/Chemistry specifics **genuinely vary by university** (UCT wants Chemistry specifically; UJ wants both Physical + Life Science)
- Pathway: BSc Geology 3yr → Honours 1yr (often required for employability) at Wits, UP, UCT, UKZN, UFS, UJ, Rhodes
- Body: SACNASP — **illegal to practice as professional geologist without registration**
- Confidence: Medium-High; subject variance by university is real, not a sourcing gap — confirm per target institution

### Environmental Scientist
- RIASEC: Investigative
- Subjects: English L4+, Mathematics L4+ (**Maths Literacy accepted at some institutions at L6 — notable exception to the "Maths not Lit" rule**), Geography L4, + L4 in Life Sciences or Physical Sciences
- Pathway: BSc Environmental Science 3-4yr → Honours 1yr recommended for SACNASP + job prospects
- Body: SACNASP
- Confidence: Medium — meaningfully lower Maths stringency than peer analytical careers; double check per university before advising a student to relax on Pure Maths, since competitive Honours programmes may still prefer it

### Biomedical Laboratory Technologist (Medical Technology)
- RIASEC: Investigative
- Subjects: English, Mathematics, Physical Sciences, Life Sciences — found minimum was L3 (40-49%), **notably low relative to peer health-science careers; weakest sourcing of this batch, treat cautiously**
- Pathway: National Diploma/BHSc-equivalent in Biomedical/Medical Laboratory Science 3-4yr (CPUT, DUT, TUT, MUT) → HPCSA Student Medical Technologist registration → ~14mo structured lab training → SMLTSA board exam → qualified Medical Technologist
- Body: HPCSA (Medical Technology Board); SMLTSA (administers board exam)
- Confidence: **Low — verify against a specific institution's current prospectus before using this L3 figure**

### Software Developer
- RIASEC: Investigative
- Subjects: Mathematics (Pure) core requirement; Physical Sciences often recommended; school IT/CAT useful not mandatory
- Pathway: BSc Computer Science or BSc/BCom IT/Informatics 3yr (widely offered) — **unlike most careers on this list, a degree is not strictly legally required**; diplomas, bootcamps, self-teaching are real alternative routes, though a BSc is the standard recommended path for a school-leaver
- Body: None mandatory; IITPSA voluntary certification
- Confidence: High — relatively unregulated field, flexible pathway

### Physicist/Astronomer
- RIASEC: Investigative
- Subjects: Mathematics + Physical Sciences at strong levels described as essential across sources; **exact university-specific subject/APS cutoffs not found this session** — general BSc figures (e.g. Maths L6/70%, Physical Science L5/60%, APS ~31) are a reasonable proxy but unconfirmed for Physics/Astronomy specifically
- Pathway: BSc Physics 3yr (Wits, UCT, UP, UKZN, Stellenbosch, UJ, Rhodes) → Honours (essentially required) → MSc/PhD for research roles. NASSP (National Astrophysics and Space Science Programme, incl. SAAO) is the standard Honours/MSc route for astronomy specifically. NRF/SAAO/SKA bursaries available.
- Body: No single mandatory licensing body; SACNASP available/relevant
- Confidence: **Low-Medium on exact figures — verify against target university's Faculty of Science page**

### Statistician
- RIASEC: Investigative
- Subjects: Mathematics L5-7 depending on programme, Mathematical Literacy not accepted; e.g. BSc Mathematical Statistics APS ~34/Maths L6 vs BCom Statistics & Data Science APS ~32/Maths L5 (both UP examples)
- Pathway: BSc/BCom Statistics/Mathematical Statistics 3yr (Wits, UP, UCT, Stellenbosch, UKZN) → Honours 1yr (practical entry point to "statistician" title, esp. for Stats SA/government roles)
- Body: SASA (South African Statistical Association) exists — **its role as a mandatory licensing body vs voluntary association was NOT confirmed this session, flag as unverified**
- Confidence: Medium on subjects (UP-specific, cross-check other universities); **Low on SASA's regulatory status**

## Sector 3: Creative / Expressive (Artistic)

**Overarching finding for this whole sector:** almost none of these careers have
legally-mandated NSC subjects (Architecture is the sole exception with a hard Maths
gate). What matters most is NSC pass *type* (Bachelor's vs Diploma/Higher Certificate
endorsement) plus, for most of these fields, a **portfolio or audition** — which is
often more decisive than subject marks. Vula's messaging should make this explicit so
students don't over-index on subject choice and under-invest in a portfolio/audition
piece.

### Graphic/UX Designer
- RIASEC: Artistic (+ Investigative for UX)
- Subjects: No strict requirement; Visual Arts, CAT, IT, or Design (CAPS subject) advantageous, not mandatory
- Pathway: BDes 3-4yr (Inscape) or National Diploma in Graphic Design 3yr (UoT), or private colleges (Vega, Red & Yellow, CityVarsity) — portfolio required. **UX has no dedicated formal SA pathway** — typically graphic design/CS graduates transition via bootcamps/self-study
- Body: None statutory
- Confidence: High for graphic design; Medium for UX — flag as a newer, less standardized field locally

### Architect
- RIASEC: Artistic + Investigative
- Subjects: **Pure Mathematics required, Maths Literacy explicitly NOT accepted** at all 6 SA architecture schools (confirmed at UCT: 50% English + 50% Pure Maths minimum). Physical Science/EGD helpful, not mandatory.
- Pathway: BAS 3yr → BAS Honours 1yr (6mo work experience) → March [Professional] 1yr → 2yr professional experience → Professional Practice Exam. Offered at UCT, Wits, UP, UJ, UFS, NMU.
- Body: **SACAP — statutory, illegal to practice/call yourself "architect" without registration**
- Confidence: High — one of the best-documented, most consistent pathways researched; the one creative career with a genuine hard legal gate

### Journalist/Media & Content
- RIASEC: Enterprising + Artistic
- Subjects: No fixed list; **English proficiency is the critical gatekeeper** (e.g. DUT diploma: English Code 5/60-69%)
- Pathway: National Diploma in Journalism 3yr (UoT) or Bachelor of Journalism/BA Journalism (DUT, NWU, Rhodes); private colleges (Boston Media House); new Journalism Occupational Qualification (NQF5-6, workplace-based) for school-leavers
- Body: None statutory — journalism is not a regulated profession; SANEF is industry advocacy, not licensing
- Confidence: High on English being central; note requirements vary meaningfully diploma vs degree vs private college

### Musician
- RIASEC: Artistic
- Subjects: NSC Music (CAPS subject) advantageous/sometimes required, **OR** external music theory exam (Unisa Grade 5/ABRSM Grade 6/Trinity) accepted as substitute — a notable non-CAPS credential pathway
- Pathway: Bachelor of Music 3-4yr (UCT SACM, Unisa, Wits) — audition/practical assessment typically required; **many working musicians, especially in popular genres, have no formal qualification at all**
- Body: None statutory
- Confidence: Medium-High for formal pathway; explicitly flag to students that formal qualification is optional in this field

### Actor/Performer
- RIASEC: Artistic
- Subjects: **No strict requirement** — Dramatic Arts helpful, not required; **audition is the decisive gatekeeper**, not subject choice
- Pathway: BA Theatre and Performance 3yr (Wits: APS 34, 60% English, compulsory audition) or AFDA (NSC Bachelor's OR Diploma/Higher Certificate pass, or AFDA's own entrance exam) — audition-based entry is the norm
- Body: None statutory
- Confidence: High that audition trumps subject choice

### Film & TV Production
- RIASEC: Artistic + Enterprising
- Subjects: **No specific subjects required** (confirmed at AFDA); for degree route, NSC Bachelor's pass, 20-24 APS, English L4+
- Pathway: AFDA Higher Certificate (1yr, lower bar) through BA (3yr); CityVarsity Diploma — multiple entry tiers, portfolio not always required at entry (unlike acting)
- Body: None statutory
- Confidence: High — consistent across sources

### Interior Designer
- RIASEC: Artistic + Conventional
- Subjects: Visual Arts/Design/EGD recommended; **Mathematics L3 (40%+) OR Mathematics Literacy L5 (60-69%) — Maths Lit accepted here, unlike Architecture, but at a notably higher required %**. Portfolio + interview + drawing test typically required.
- Pathway: Diploma in Interior Design 3yr (DUT, TUT, CPUT) or BDes (Inscape)
- Body: IID (Institute of Interior Design Professions) — voluntary but reputationally important title protection, not a legal practice requirement
- Confidence: Medium-High; Maths Lit % figure is indicative, verify per institution

### Fashion Designer
- RIASEC: Artistic (+ Enterprising for the business side)
- Subjects: **No subject requirement, including no Art requirement** — confirmed directly via LISOF's own admissions messaging (matric + passion is the stated bar)
- Pathway: Higher Certificate (1yr) through Diploma/BA/BCom (3yr) at LISOF, STADIO, CPUT, private colleges
- Body: None statutory
- Confidence: High — unusually well-confirmed directly by an institution

### Photographer
- RIASEC: Artistic
- Subjects: No strict requirement; Visual Arts/Maths/Physical Sciences/Design cited as generally complementary
- Pathway: Highly variable — general/freelance photography can be self-taught (no formal qualification legally needed); specialized paths (photojournalism, commercial, scientific) typically want a diploma/degree (DUT Bachelor of Applied Arts in Commercial Photography, NMU Photography major)
- Body: PSSA (Photographic Society of SA) — voluntary, not a licensing requirement; **no confirmed mandatory registration regime for photographers in SA**
- Confidence: Medium-High — one of the least "gatekept" careers researched; flag formal qualification as optional/helpful, not required

### Animator/Game Designer
- RIASEC: Artistic + Investigative
- Subjects: No absolute requirement, but **this is the creative field where Mathematics is most consistently relevant** given the technical/programming component — figures found range from "compulsory" (some private colleges, e.g. 40% Maths or 60% Maths Lit/Technical Maths) to merely "preferred" (Wits BA Digital Arts/Game Design: Maths/Physical Sciences at 60%+ preferred, NOT compulsory — portfolio + motivation letter required instead)
- Pathway: Split paths — Animation-focused (STADIO Bachelor of Applied Arts in 3D Animation, The Animation School Diploma) vs Game-Design-focused (Wits BA Digital Arts, Vega BCIS Game Design, Boston Higher Certificate) — portfolio and/or entrance test typically required across all routes
- Body: None statutory
- Confidence: Medium — widest spread in stated Maths requirements found in this sector; advise checking per specific institution

## Sector 4: Caring / People-focused (Social)

### Teacher
- RIASEC: Social
- Subjects: NSC Bachelor's pass + English. **Mathematics vs Maths Literacy is phase-dependent**: Foundation Phase (Gr R-3) accepts Maths Lit L4; Intermediate Phase (Gr 4-6) recommends Mathematics L4+; **Senior/FET Phase teaching Mathematics specifically requires the teacher to have taken Pure Mathematics themselves in matric** — cannot become a high school Maths teacher having done Maths Lit. Two "priority subjects" generally needed at L4 (50%+) for Funza Lushaka funding.
- Pathway: 4yr BEd (any of 26 public universities) in Foundation/Intermediate/Senior & FET Phase specialization, or 1yr PGCE (postgrad, on top of another degree — **not Funza Lushaka-funded for 2026**). Funza Lushaka bursary covers full costs in exchange for teaching service (1yr service per 1yr funded), contingent on criminal record/child-offender clearance.
- Body: SACE (South African Council for Educators) — mandatory registration
- Confidence: High on the Maths vs Maths Lit distinction for teaching Maths specifically — high-impact rule; verify priority-subject list and bursary terms yearly on funzalushaka.doe.gov.za

### Nurse
- RIASEC: Social
- Subjects: English L4 (50%+); Life Sciences L4 (50%+) typically; **Mathematics requirement varies significantly by institution** — UJ requires Pure Maths L4 and explicitly rejects Maths Lit; UKZN accepts either at L3; NMU accepts either at different thresholds (50% Maths OR 65% Maths Lit)
- Pathway: 4yr Bachelor of Nursing (BNurs/BCur) at public university → Professional/Registered Nurse; diploma routes exist via nursing colleges (2020 restructured SANC framework)
- Body: SANC (South African Nursing Council) — mandatory to practise in any capacity
- Confidence: Medium-High; **the Maths/Maths Lit inconsistency across universities means default advice should be "take Pure Maths if possible" to keep all doors open**

### Social Worker
- RIASEC: Social
- Subjects: English L4 (50%+, some universities want 60%). **Mathematics requirements notably lenient** — e.g. UMP accepts Maths L2 or Maths Lit L3. No science subjects typically needed — a Humanities-track career.
- Pathway: 4yr Bachelor of Social Work (BSW, direct professional degree, not generic BA+honours) — 2nd-year students register as Student Social Workers with SACSSP
- Body: SACSSP (South African Council for Social Service Professions) — mandatory to use the protected title and practise
- Confidence: Good confidence on core message (Maths Lit fine, no sciences needed); exact % thresholds vary 50-60% by university

### Psychologist
- RIASEC: Social + Investigative
- Subjects: English required; **Mathematics OR Mathematics Literacy both generally accepted** — unlike most health sciences. E.g. BPsych: L5 (60-69%) in six subjects incl. English and Maths-or-Lit.
- Pathway: **This is the longest, most misunderstood pathway on the list.** Full "Psychologist" registration: 3yr Bachelor's (Psychology major) → Honours 1yr (competitive selection) → 2yr Master's (clinical/counselling/educational — extremely competitive selection) → 12mo internship → (Clinical only) extra community service year → National Board Exam → HPCSA registration. Total ~6-7 years. **Shorter alternative: 4yr BPsych professional degree** (incl. 720hr practicum) → National Exam → register as **Registered Counsellor** (a distinct, lower scope-of-practice category — NOT "Psychologist").
- Body: HPCSA, Professional Board for Psychology
- Confidence: High on subjects. **Critical flag: the "Psychologist" vs "Registered Counsellor" distinction is the single biggest area students/parents misunderstand** — many believe a bachelor's degree alone makes you a psychologist, which is false. Vula should surface Registered Counsellor as a realistic shorter alternative rather than implying the full pathway is the only outcome.

### Occupational Therapist
- RIASEC: Social + Investigative
- Subjects: Mathematics ~50% (or Maths Lit ~60%, varies); 50%+ in Physical Sciences OR Life Sciences (**institutions genuinely differ on whether both, either, or one is required** — UKZN accepts either; Stellenbosch wants both at 50%); English 50%+
- Pathway: 4yr Bachelor of Occupational Therapy (UCT, Wits, UP, Stellenbosch, UKZN, SMU, UWC, UFS) — min 1000 clinical hours required by HPCSA before graduation; + 1yr community service
- Body: HPCSA, Professional Board for OT/Medical Orthotics-Prosthetics/Arts Therapy
- Confidence: Medium — Stellenbosch/UP pages 403'd during research; cross-check exact figures per target university, especially the Physical vs Life Science either/or/both question

### Physiotherapist
- RIASEC: Social + Investigative
- Subjects: **Strictest science requirements of the allied health professions** — Mathematics (L4-5, 50-60%+), Physical Sciences (L4, 50%+), AND Life Sciences (L4, 50%+) — all three commonly required, not optional. Stellenbosch: aggregate 60%+, Maths 60%, Physical Sciences 50%, Life Sciences L4. Wits: Maths L5, Life/Physical Sciences L5.
- Pathway: 4yr Bachelor of Physiotherapy/BSc Physiotherapy (UCT, Wits, UP, Stellenbosch, UWC, SMU, UKZN) + 1yr community service
- Body: HPCSA, Professional Board for Physiotherapy/Podiatry/Biokinetics
- Confidence: High — well-documented, consistently strict across institutions; safe to tell students this needs all three sciences at strong levels (aim 60%+), not Maths Lit

### Speech Therapist (Speech-Language Pathology)
- RIASEC: Social + Investigative
- Subjects: Strong English + Life Sciences (~60%+ commonly cited); Mathematics requirement varies by institution — not confirmed consistently. **Additional/African language competence is valuable given SA's multilingual clinical context** (taught within the degree), though not confirmed as a hard NSC-level prerequisite anywhere. APS ~32-34 typical.
- Pathway: 4yr professional Bachelor's degree (**a BA at some institutions despite being clinical/scientific** — can surprise students) at UP, Wits, UCT, Stellenbosch, SMU. Students register as student clinicians with HPCSA during study; + 1yr community service.
- Body: HPCSA, Professional Board for Speech, Language and Hearing Professions
- Confidence: Medium — exact Maths requirement not consistently confirmed across sources, verify per institution

### Paramedic / Emergency Medical Care
- RIASEC: Social + Realistic
- Subjects: Mathematics + Life Sciences + Physical Sciences, generally minimum L3 (30-39%) for base entry though competitive programmes want higher; APS cited 24-26. **Also requires medical/physical fitness and (some institutions) swimming proficiency** — non-academic barriers matter here too.
- Pathway: Genuinely tiered — (1) Higher Certificate in EMC (1yr) → Basic Ambulance Assistant; (2) Diploma in EMC (2-3yr, UJ/CPUT/Netcare Education/Lebone College) → registers as Paramedic; (3) some universities now offer Bachelor's route. TVET N4 alternative entry exists.
- Body: HPCSA, Professional Board for Emergency Care
- Confidence: Medium — tiered structure is real and important to explain; exact subject-level cutoffs varied across sources, confirm per institution (UJ page 403'd)

### Police Officer (SAPS)
- RIASEC: Social + Enterprising + Realistic
- Subjects: **No specific subject requirements at all** — must have passed English and be fluent in English + at least one other official SA language. **Least academically restrictive career on this entire list.**
- Pathway: Not a degree — direct SAPS recruitment (age 18-30, SA citizen by birth, clean record, fitness/psych assessment) → 24-month Basic Police Development Learning Programme at a SAPS training college → full appointment. Specialized units (detective, forensics) often want further study after entry.
- Body: No external council — governed internally by SAPS under the SAPS Act
- Confidence: High on core facts; age limit cited inconsistently across sources (30 vs up to 35) — direct students to the live SAPS e-recruitment portal for current criteria

### HR Practitioner
- RIASEC: Social + Enterprising
- Subjects: English L4 (50%); **Mathematics/Technical Maths L4 (50%) OR Mathematics Literacy L5 (60%)** — Maths Lit accepted but at a higher required % than pure Maths, a common pattern. Diploma-route requirements lower (40% Maths or 70% Maths Lit).
- Pathway: Tiered — Higher Certificate (1yr) → Diploma (3yr, TVET/UoT) → Bachelor's (3yr, UJ/CPUT/MUT) → often Honours for progression. Not tightly gated.
- Body: **SABPP (South African Board for People Practices) — voluntary professional credentialing, NOT a legal practice requirement**, unlike SANC/HPCSA/SACSSP/SAPS
- Confidence: Medium-High; important to communicate SABPP is optional so students don't think they're legally barred from HR work without it

### Early Childhood Development (ECD) Practitioner
- RIASEC: Social
- Subjects: No specific NSC requirements at lower tiers; entry varies drastically by target qualification (NQF4 Occupational Certificate may not need full matric at some TVET colleges; NQF5 Higher Certificate needs NSC Higher Certificate endorsement; NQF6 Diploma needs NSC Diploma endorsement, APS 25+)
- Pathway: **Genuinely tiered with a real pay/title ceiling** — NQF4/5 = "Practitioner" (lower pay, works under supervision) vs NQF6+ Diploma = eligible to be classified/paid as "Teacher" in some structures. N4-N6 Educare (TVET) or Higher Certificate/Diploma in ECD (university) — NSFAS-fundable at public TVET colleges.
- Body: **Genuinely unsettled** — ECD moved from Dept of Social Development to Dept of Basic Education on 1 April 2022; SACE registration associated with qualified ECD teachers (higher NQF) but NQF4/5 Practitioner-level classification/SACE-eligibility is described in source material as an unresolved policy tension
- Confidence: **Low-Medium on the professional-body question specifically** — present the qualification-tier/pay-ceiling distinction clearly, but avoid asserting a firm SACE registration requirement without further direct DBE/SACE confirmation

### Dietician (Dietitian)
- RIASEC: Social + Investigative
- Subjects: NSC aggregate typically 60%+; minimum 50% each in Mathematics, Physical Sciences, AND Life Sciences — all three required, similar rigor to Physiotherapy/OT
- Pathway: 4yr BSc Dietetics (Stellenbosch, UWC, Mandela University, NWU, SMU, University of Limpopo) + 1yr community service
- Body: HPCSA, Professional Board for Dietetics and Nutrition. Note: "Dietitian" is the correct professional spelling in SA usage (vs colloquial "Dietician") — Vula should be consistent.
- Confidence: High — consistent and well-corroborated

## Sector 5: Driven / Enterprising

### Entrepreneur / Business Owner
- RIASEC: Enterprising
- Subjects: No strict requirement — no formal credential pathway exists. Recommended: Business Studies, Economics, Accounting, Mathematics (Maths Lit generally acceptable, Pure Maths keeps BCom-type doors open).
- Pathway: No degree legally required to start a business. BCom/BCom Entrepreneurial Management 3yr (UJ, MANCOSA), or Higher Certificate in Entrepreneurship (1yr, NQF5) are common enabling (not mandatory) pathways.
- Body: None — unregulated (specific business activities may trigger sector-specific licensing separately)
- Confidence: High confidence there's no mandatory academic pathway; subject "helpfulness" is general guidance consensus, not a hard rule — flag this to students

### Lawyer
- RIASEC: Enterprising + Investigative
- Subjects: **No specific NSC subjects mandatory** — Careers Portal SA explicitly states no specific subjects required, though History is "an advantage." What matters is strong English + Mathematics/Maths Lit marks feeding into APS. Example: UJ APS 31 w/ Maths or 32 w/ Maths Lit; Rhodes ~60% English HL (70% FAL) + 50% Maths/60% Maths Lit.
- Pathway: LLB 4yr (straight from matric) OR 2+2 (3yr non-law degree + 2yr LLB — some universities phasing this out, check current rules). Then: candidate attorneys complete Practical Vocational Training (articles, ~2yr) OR candidate advocates complete pupillage (1yr) → Legal Practice Council competency exam → "fit and proper person" court application.
- Body: Legal Practice Council (LPC) — statutory, under the Legal Practice Act 28 of 2014
- Confidence: Medium-High; exact APS/subject-level cutoffs vary by university and year — some figures from secondary aggregators not universities' own pages, treat as indicative

### Marketing & Sales
- RIASEC: Enterprising
- Subjects: No fixed requirement, especially for sales roles (explicitly no strict academic pathway). Business Studies, Economics, Mathematics/Maths Lit recommended as helpful groundwork for marketing specifically. **One NATED N6 Marketing Management source explicitly confirms Mathematics is NOT required.**
- Pathway: Highly flexible — BCom Marketing Management 3yr (university), National Diploma/BTech (UoT), or NATED N4-N6 (TVET, ~18mo + 18mo experience). Many sales roles need only matric + on-the-job training.
- Body: None mandatory; IMM (Institute of Marketing Management) voluntary
- Confidence: High confidence there's no fixed academic pathway, especially for sales — communicate this clearly so students don't feel gatekept

### Real Estate Agent
- RIASEC: Enterprising
- Subjects: **No NSC subject is a legal prerequisite** — confirmed by two independent sources; compliance/regulatory pages make no mention of NSC subjects at all. Languages/Economics/Business Studies/Accounting helpful, not required.
- Pathway: **No degree required, but a real mandatory regulatory exam sequence**: (1) register with PPRA as Candidate Property Practitioner; (2) 12mo practical internship; (3) FETC: Real Estate NQF4 (150 credits/1500 notional hours); (4) pass PDE4 within 2yr → Non-Principal Property Practitioner. To own/run an agency (Principal): additionally NQF5 + pass PDE5 within 2yr.
- Body: PPRA (Property Practitioners Regulatory Authority), under the Property Practitioners Act 22 of 2019
- Confidence: High on the licensing pathway — well-documented, consistent across sources; good example of "no academic gate, but strict professional licensing"

### Public Administration / Politics
- RIASEC: Enterprising
- Subjects: No fixed list found; NSC Bachelor's pass generally needed for the administration-degree route; English proficiency consistently required (40-50% language of teaching). **Could not find a source specifically prescribing subjects for this career — genuine sourcing gap.**
- Pathway: BA Public Administration/Governance or Bachelor of Administration 3yr (UKZN, NWU, MANCOSA, Stadio) for the civil-service track. **"Politics" as elected office has NO formal qualification requirement at all** — entry via party politics/activism/community leadership, a meaningfully different pathway from public-administration study and should be flagged as such.
- Body: No single regulator; Public Service Commission oversees standards but doesn't license individuals
- Confidence: Lower than other entries — inferred general guidance, not sourced from a specific SA authority; state plainly to students that "politics" has literally no academic gate

### Hospitality Manager
- RIASEC: Enterprising
- Subjects: No mandatory subjects, but **Tourism and/or Hospitality Studies are real, distinct NSC/CAPS subjects** directly supporting this pathway. English, Maths/Maths Lit, Geography, Life Sciences cited as beneficial by one source.
- Pathway: Diploma in Hospitality Management 3yr, NQF6 (DUT, UMP) — NSC Diploma endorsement, L4 (50%+) English, L3 (40%+) Maths or L4 (50%+) Maths Lit. TVET hospitality subjects/CATHSSETA routes also exist.
- Body: None individual; CATHSSETA (Culture, Arts, Tourism, Hospitality and Sport SETA) for training accreditation, not practitioner licensing
- Confidence: Medium-High on the diploma pathway; entry marks are institution-specific examples

### Event Manager
- RIASEC: Enterprising
- Subjects: No strict requirement. Business Studies, Tourism/Hospitality Studies, Geography, English suggested as useful groundwork (general tourism-sector sourcing, not event-management-specific).
- Pathway: Diploma/Higher Certificate in Event Management (Boston City Campus, MANCOSA, UMP — NQF5 1yr or NQF6 ~3yr). NSC with 40%+ in four 20-credit subjects + 30%+ English typical for diploma entry. No degree strictly required — many enter via short courses/on-the-job experience.
- Body: None — unregulated profession, no licensing found
- Confidence: Medium — no authoritative government/regulator source exists since the field is unregulated; entry marks are private-college-specific and will vary

### Insurance Broker
- RIASEC: Enterprising
- Subjects: No fixed list; Mathematics, Accounting, Business Studies, Economics logically supportive (BCom-feeding) but not formally required anywhere in official FSCA material found.
- Pathway: Short route — Higher Certificate in Short-Term Insurance/Risk Management (NQF5, ~1yr); or degree route — BCom Insurance and Risk Management (3-4yr). **Regardless of route, all representatives must hold an FSCA-recognized qualification AND pass the RE5 exam (50 MCQs, need 66%) within 2yr of first appointment.** Key Individuals/brokerage owners may need RE1 additionally — not fully verified this session.
- Body: FSCA (Financial Sector Conduct Authority), under the FAIS Act; Insurance Institute of SA (voluntary, Associateship/Fellowship)
- Confidence: High on RE5 requirement and 2yr deadline; Medium on full qualification list (FSCA's recognized-qualifications list changes — direct to fsca.co.za for current list); "broker" vs "representative" regulatory tiers need follow-up if advising about owning a brokerage specifically

### Import/Export Trader
- RIASEC: Enterprising
- Subjects: No fixed requirement found; Business Studies, Economics, Maths/Maths Lit, and a foreign Language logically useful, not prescribed by any SA source found.
- Pathway: No mandatory degree. 3yr Diploma in International Trade Management (CHE-accredited), or UNISA's International Freight Management programme (shorter, non-degree), or short courses (UCT Import/Export/Logistics). Many enter via general BCom/Logistics + on-the-job experience. **Businesses must register as importers/exporters with SARS Customs** to legally trade across borders (business-level requirement, not individual license — not independently verified with a SARS source this session).
- Body: No individual licensing; ITRISA (Institute of Export in SA) voluntary professional body
- Confidence: Lower-Medium — genuinely under-documented from a SA schools/careers-guidance perspective; only training-provider pages found, no authoritative government/careers-portal source

### Tour Operator
- RIASEC: Enterprising
- Subjects: No fixed requirement. English, Languages, History, Geography recommended by one SA-specific source (postmatric.co.za); Tourism (CAPS subject) also directly relevant.
- Pathway: No degree required — flexible. TVET National Certificate in Tourism (N4-N6); private tourist-guiding/travel-ops certificates; or a degree (Tourism/Hospitality/Marketing/Business) for a competitive edge, not mandatory. **Important distinction: "Tour Operator" (business/logistics role, no registration required) is a DIFFERENT job from "Tourist Guide" (leads tours in person, requires CATHSSETA-accredited training + mandatory provincial registration, e.g. via Western Cape Government)** — do not conflate these in advice.
- Body: No mandatory registration for the Tour Operator business role itself; Tourist Guides specifically require registration
- Confidence: Medium; the Operator-vs-Guide distinction is an important accuracy point to get right

## Sector 6: Organised / Detail-focused (Conventional)

### Accountant / Chartered Accountant
- RIASEC: Conventional
- Subjects: **Mathematics (Pure, NOT Maths Lit) minimum L5 (60%+)** — confirmed directly via SAICA's own page; English strong pass recommended; Accounting as NSC subject helpful but explicitly optional.
- Pathway: BCom Accounting (SAICA-accredited) 3yr → CTA/PGDA 1yr → 3yr SAICA training contract ("articles") → ITC + APC board exams → CA(SA). ~7yr total. Shorter SAIPA route (BCom + 3yr traineeship) → Professional Accountant (SA) for non-CA accounting roles.
- Body: SAICA (Chartered Accountants) or SAIPA (Professional Accountants)
- Confidence: High — SAICA's own page confirms the Maths L5/60% minimum and optional Accounting-as-subject

### Banking & Finance
- RIASEC: Conventional
- Subjects: Mathematics preferred (50%+); some BCom programmes accept Maths Lit at 70%+ with conditional/bridging admission if strong in Economics/Accounting/Physical Science
- Pathway: **Spans matric-level to degree-level roles** — BCom (Banking/Investment/Financial Management) for management track, OR NQF4 Senior Certificate + BANKSETA learnership (Letsema) for entry-level teller/administrator roles (degree not always required at entry level)
- Body: IOBSA (Institute of Bankers in SA) — tiered voluntary designations BP(SA) up to FIBSA
- Confidence: Medium — treat "Banking & Finance" as a spectrum, requirements diverge significantly by role level, not one fixed pathway

### Logistics & Supply Chain
- RIASEC: Conventional
- Subjects: Mathematics preferred, not always mandatory (some diplomas accept 40%+ in four 20-credit subjects incl. English); Accounting/Statistics advantageous
- Pathway: Higher Certificate/Diploma in Logistics/Supply Chain (TVET or private, e.g. Rosebank, Boston) for entry roles; BCom Logistics/Supply Chain (e.g. STADIO) for management track
- Body: SAPICS (CPIM/CSCP/CLTD certifications) and CILT South Africa (CMILT designation) — both voluntary
- Confidence: Medium — no single fixed NSC standard, varies by institution and diploma vs degree track

### Bookkeeper
- RIASEC: Conventional
- Subjects: No strict requirement; Mathematics recommended not mandatory
- Pathway: **ICB (Institute of Certified Bookkeepers) is the realistic route and notably does NOT require matric** for entry levels — National Certificate (Grade 10+, age 16+) → Further Education Certificate (NQF3) → National Certificate: Bookkeeping (NQF5, matric-equivalent) → Certified Junior/Senior Bookkeeper. Matric + short course also common in practice.
- Body: ICB; CIBA (Chartered Institute for Business Accountants) also offers a bookkeeper pathway
- Confidence: High on the "matric not strictly required" point — multiple independent sources confirm ICB's tiered, non-matric-gated entry

### Payroll Administrator
- RIASEC: Conventional
- Subjects: No universally published NSC list; "matric with a bachelor's pass including Maths" is a common employer preference, **not a fixed SAPA rule**
- Pathway: Matric + Certificate/Diploma in Payroll Administration (NQF4-5, SETA-accredited providers) is the realistic entry path; larger employers may prefer a diploma/degree in HR, Business Admin, or Accounting
- Body: SAPA (South African Payroll Association) — offers a Professionalisation framework, "preferable" not always mandatory
- Confidence: Medium-low on specific subjects — no authoritative SAPA statement found mandating specific NSC subjects; flag that Mathematics helps but isn't a formal gate

### Municipal/Government Administrator
- RIASEC: Conventional
- Subjects: No uniform prescribed combination — institutions set their own. For Diploma in Public Administration (UNISA): ~50% language of instruction + 40% Mathematics OR 70% Maths Lit. **For entry-level municipal admin clerk jobs, Grade 12 (any stream) is often the only formal requirement.**
- Pathway: **Spans entry clerk (matric only) through Municipal Manager (degree + 5+yr senior experience)** — Diploma or BA/BCom/BAdmin in Public Administration (UNISA, MANCOSA, Mandela University) needed for progression toward supervisory/management roles.
- Body: No single mandatory body for administrators generally; SALGA is sectoral, not licensing; IMFO relevant for municipal finance officers specifically
- Confidence: Medium — this career spans a huge range; make sure Vula's advice specifies which level (entry clerk vs management) it means

### Court Clerk
- RIASEC: Conventional
- Subjects: **No specific NSC subjects required beyond matric itself** — confirmed across multiple current (2025/2026) Department of Justice/Office of the Chief Justice vacancy postings
- Pathway: Matric (Grade 12) is the minimum and often sufficient — current OCJ postings explicitly state "no previous work experience required." A 3yr law-related diploma/degree (NQF6) is advantageous, not mandatory, for entry posts. Progression to Registrar/higher legal-admin roles benefits from paralegal or LLB study.
- Body: None mandatory for entry-level Court/Administration Clerk — DPSA-graded public service posts, not a licensed profession
- Confidence: High on "matric only, any subjects" — corroborated by live 2025/2026 government vacancy postings (official DOJ careers brochure PDF could not be reliably parsed, so this rests on current vacancy notices rather than that document directly)

### Librarian
- RIASEC: Conventional
- Subjects: NSC with minimum achievement rating 3 in four 20-credit subjects + English rating 3 (standard diploma-level entry); Mathematics/Languages/CAT recommended, not mandatory at high levels
- Pathway: Diploma or Bachelor's in Library and Information Science (UCT, DUT, UNISA Postgraduate Diploma) is the standard professional route; a lower-tier "Library Assistant" occupational certificate (QCTO) exists for paraprofessional roles without a full LIS degree
- Body: LIASA (Library and Information Association of SA) — professional membership/registration post-qualification
- Confidence: Medium-high; general entry-level requirements clear, exact figures vary by specific university (UCT vs DUT vs UNISA)

### Quantity Surveyor
- RIASEC: Conventional (+ Realistic)
- Subjects: **Mathematics essential (Pure, not Lit)** — UP BSc Quantity Surveying requires English L5, Mathematics L5, Physical Sciences L4, APS 30 (2025/26 entry). Bridging "ENGAGE" pathway exists at lower APS 25 (still L5 Maths + L4 Physical Science + NBT). Other universities' minimums vary — some accept Maths L4.
- Pathway: BSc/BTech Quantity Surveying (SACQSP-accredited: UP, UCT, CUT, Wits) → min 3yr practical training (≥2yr under a registered QS) → Assessment of Professional Competence (APC) → registration as Professional Quantity Surveyor (PrQS). Non-accredited degrees need 11 extra professional-skills modules before APC eligibility.
- Body: **SACQSP (statutory registration body)**; ASAQS (voluntary professional association); RICS (optional international accreditation, not the primary SA regulator)
- Confidence: High — UP's official programme page gives precise current subject levels; cross-check against other universities since some accept Maths L4 not L5
