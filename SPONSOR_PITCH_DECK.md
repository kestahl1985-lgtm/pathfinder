## Reach the right students, before they've made up their mind.

**Vula** is a free, WhatsApp-native career-guidance tool, open to anyone in South Africa seeking career direction. In under 5 minutes they answer 30 tappable questions and get a personalised career profile, a downloadable PDF report, and — where relevant — see your courses surfaced as the match for their interests and province.

## The problem you already know

Most SA schools have one career counsellor for hundreds of learners, if any at all. Learners lock in Grade 9/10 subject choices with almost no real information about what those choices open or close off. By the time your recruitment team reaches them in Grade 12, many have already ruled themselves out of your programmes without realising it.

## How it works for you

- A learner completes their profile on WhatsApp — no app, no login, works on any phone.
- If their RIASEC profile and province match your programme, your course is surfaced directly inside their results, at the exact moment they're exploring that career path.
- You're reaching prospective students by genuine interest fit and geography — not buying a generic list and hoping it lands.

## Pricing

**Billed annually — 20% off the monthly rate.**

| Package | Reach | Annual | Monthly |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Faculty / single programme** | One faculty or programme, in one province | **R18,000**/year | R1,875/month |
| **Provincial** | Learners in one province | **R48,000**/year | R5,000/month |
| **National** | Learners in all nine provinces | **R192,000**/year | R20,000/month |

Your courses are matched by interest profile and province. Where two sponsors match someone comparably, the one who has reached fewer people in the last 30 days is shown — nobody is starved by signing second, and a weak match is never shown just for fairness.

**What we commit to in writing:** a minimum floor scaled to your tier — **150** Faculty, **400** Provincial, **1,600** National learners reached, counted as distinct people rather than page views. That is **R120 per learner reached at every tier**. Fall short and your term extends free of charge (clause 3.5). Founding sponsors can also cancel at day 90 with a pro-rata refund if we have not reached a quarter of that floor (clause 3.6).

We also cap how many sponsors we take per province. Placements rotate through a finite pool, so overselling a province would put every existing floor at risk — including yours.

*Why national is 4× provincial and not 9×:* it buys 4.2× the audience of KwaZulu-Natal, the largest province, for 4.0× the price. The top three provinces bought separately cost R144,000 for 56% of the market.

**Founding sponsors** *(first 5–10 only)* — your rate held at renewal, first claim on your province, logo placement on the Vula marketing site, and first right of refusal on a joint case study.

*Excl. VAT, payable annually in advance. Monthly billing available at the rates shown.*

## What the learners are choosing into

- **60.9%** of South Africans aged 15–24 are unemployed ([Stats SA, QLFS Q1 2026](https://www.statssa.gov.za/?p=19526)).
- **10.1%** absorption rate for that age group — the share actually in work.
- **2.9 million** learners are picking subjects that open or close those doors years early.

Vula is free to everyone who uses it, permanently — no app, no data cost, no fee. Sponsorship is what pays for that. A sponsor funds career guidance for people who have none, and reaches the ones whose interests genuinely fit their programmes. Those are the same act, which is the point of the model.

*Placements are matched algorithmically and impression volume is not guaranteed — see clause 3.3 of the sponsorship agreement. The minimum impression floor in clause 3.5 is how that risk is shared.*

## Why this is lower-risk than a data buy

Vula's model is sponsorship, not lead sales — deliberately. You get visibility with the right audience without becoming a second custodian of minors' personal information. That's a POPIA exposure most institutions would rather not take on, and Vula's structure means you never have to.
