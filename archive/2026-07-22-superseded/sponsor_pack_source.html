1 — The case

Reach the right students, before they've made up their mind.

Vula is a free, WhatsApp-native career-guidance tool for South African Grade 10–12 learners. In under five minutes a learner answers 30 tappable questions and gets a personalised career profile, a downloadable report, and — where it genuinely fits — your courses shown as the match for their interests and province.

The problem you already know

Most South African schools have one career counsellor for hundreds of learners, if any at all. Learners lock in Grade 9 and 10 subject choices with almost no real information about what those choices open or close off.

By the time your recruitment team reaches them in Grade 12, many have already ruled themselves out of your programmes without ever knowing it.

60.9%
of South Africans aged 15–24 are unemployed. Subject choices made at fifteen are one of the earliest points at which that outcome is shaped. Stats SA, Quarterly Labour Force Survey, Q1 2026

How it works for you

  1. A learner completes their profile on WhatsApp — no app, no login, no data bundle to speak of. It works on any phone.
  2. Their interest profile and province are matched against your programme. Where you fit, your course appears inside their results at the moment they're exploring that career.
  3. You reach students by genuine interest and geography — not by buying a list and hoping it lands.

What you get

Targeted placement. Shown only to learners whose profile and province actually match what you offer.

Brand presence at the decision point. Your name and course details in front of a learner while they are still choosing, not after.

Simple reporting. Impression counts per course, so you can see reach without ever touching learner-level data.

No new data liability. Vula never hands you a learner's personal information.

Why this is lower-risk than buying data

Vula's model is sponsorship, not lead sales — deliberately. You get visibility with the right audience without becoming a second custodian of minors' personal information. That is a POPIA exposure most institutions would rather not take on, and Vula's structure means you never have to.

Vula is free to every learner, permanently — no app, no data cost, no fee. Sponsorship is what pays for that. Funding career guidance for learners whose schools cannot provide it, and reaching the ones whose interests genuinely fit your programmes, are the same act. That is the point of the model.

Two things worth knowing about this audience

It thins quickly. There are 1,187,468 learners in Grade 10 but only 740,566 in Grade 12 — 38% fewer. The learners a sponsor most wants to reach are the ones still deciding, and they are most numerous at the start of the phase.

It concentrates. KwaZulu-Natal, Gauteng and Limpopo together hold 56% of the national total. A provincial package in one of those three reaches a materially larger pool than the other six.

These are enrolment figures, not Vula's audience.

They describe how large the addressable market is in each province. Vula is pre-launch and its own reach today is a waitlist. Sponsor reporting shows impressions actually delivered to real learners — never market size.
